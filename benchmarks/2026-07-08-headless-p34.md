# Benchmark 3 — headless, unbiased, n=3 per arm (via P3.4)

First run under the harness (`benchmarks/harness/`): fresh worktrees, one
headless `claude -p` per rep, auto permission mode, independent scoring,
measured `total_cost_usd`. No inline orchestration, no contaminated
experimenter, no estimated costs. Same task as benchmark 2 (chunked
resumable uploads, 6 named acceptance tests).

Arms: **byproxy** = v2 skill, orchestrator pinned claude-fable-5 @ low
effort, explorers/builders per agent definitions (haiku/sonnet).
**solo** = claude-opus-4-8, bare prompt.

## Results

| rep | byproxy cost | byproxy wall | solo cost | solo wall |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | $4.79 | 16.7 min | $5.80 | 13.5 min |
| 2 | $4.92 | 17.6 min | $3.19 | 6.7 min |
| 3 | $4.94 | 17.3 min | $4.19 | 11.8 min |
| **mean** | **$4.88** | **17.2 min** | **$4.40** | **10.7 min** |

**Completion: 6/6 runs fully green** (all 6 tests + vet + full race suite,
independently replayed). Zero permission denials in any run.

byproxy per-model split (consistent across reps): fable orchestrator
$2.41–2.69 (~53%), sonnet builders $1.56–1.94, haiku explorers $0.57–0.65.
Orchestrator turns: 13–15 per run (vs solo's 42–58).

## Verdict

**Cost: statistical tie.** Solo's mean is 10% lower, but its range
($3.19–$5.80) fully spans byproxy's. n=3 cannot separate them.
**Wall time: solo wins clearly (~1.6×).** **Quality: no separation on
pass/fail** — the task's DoD may be too achievable to discriminate.

Two genuine byproxy differentiators showed up anyway:

1. **Cost predictability.** byproxy's spread was $0.15 across reps; solo's
   was $2.61. The structured pipeline buys a tight cost envelope — worth
   real money to anyone budgeting fleets, and invisible in a mean.
2. **Self-auditing output.** Every byproxy run's final report disclosed
   known limitations its own audit found (e.g. chunk re-POST double-counts
   received bytes; failed assembly kills the partial). Solo reported
   success without caveats. Same green tests — different epistemic states
   for the human reading the report. One byproxy rep also implemented and
   tested the progress Signal the DoD didn't require but the roadmap did;
   the inline runs (and solo) had silently de-scoped it.

The headless orchestrator followed the v2 workflow unaided (recon →
design red-team → build → audit) and made independent, reasonable judgment
calls (single-unit decomposition; extending the vt harness when it needed
raw session-bearing requests). SKILL.md works without a human compensating
for it — that was benchmark 3's real question.

## The next lever is the orchestrator's price tag

The v2 redesign fixed the data plane: explore+build now costs ~$2.1–2.5
(benchmark 2's inline equivalent was several times that). The dominant
line item is the control plane itself — fable at low effort is terse
(13–15 turns) but premium-priced per token. Projection: the same runs with
a sonnet orchestrator land at **~$2.6–3.2, clearly under the solo mean**.
That ablation (and a harder task whose DoD actually discriminates quality)
is benchmark 4.

## Corrections to earlier benchmarks

- Benchmarks 1–2 were inline-orchestrated: contaminated orchestrator,
  estimated orchestrator cost, single samples, experimenter steering.
  Directionally useful (they motivated v2), not evidence. This harness
  supersedes their methodology.
- Benchmark 2's "solo wins L-tasks" conclusion does not replicate under
  v2: the arms now tie on cost with solo ahead on wall time only.
- The "quadratic context cost" intuition died completely: solo's runs
  served ~97% of input from prompt cache (4.97M cache-read tokens in one
  rep). Context growth is cheap; turns are not.
